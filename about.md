I'm a Data Scientist at NatWest Group in London, where I work within Treasury Markets. My work sits at the intersection of data science, quantitative finance, and financial markets — building the systems and analytical tools that support institutional decision making at scale.

Day to day, this means treasury market analytics, quantitative valuation, pricing and IPV automation, and data products. I'm drawn to the places where mathematical rigour meets engineering reality: where a model has to be correct *and* reliable *and* fast enough to be useful in production.

Before NatWest, I completed an MSc in Data Science at the London School of Economics, and a BSc in Finance, Accounting and Management at the University of Nottingham. The combination of a quantitative finance background and data science training is what I think of as my core advantage — I understand the structure of financial problems, and I know how to build systems to solve them.

## What I'm interested in

- Foundation models and their applications in structured, tabular, and financial data
- Quantitative methods for pricing, valuation, and risk
- Data engineering and real-time analytics infrastructure
- Financial markets, macro, and the mechanics of price discovery
- Early-stage technology, product thinking, and what makes software genuinely useful
- Long-term compounding — in investing, in craft, and in knowledge

## Background

I grew up between cultures, which shaped how I think: attentive to context, comfortable with ambiguity, and drawn to systems thinking. Finance and data both reward this disposition.

I read widely — history of science, economics, philosophy of mind, and anything that helps me think more clearly about complex systems. I'm a long-term investor with genuine interest in capital allocation and business quality.
